Auto-Lorebook · Pipeline Stage 1b

Refining Fact Extraction

Teaching the reading stage to capture setting lore — and ignore the actual-play session's own play-by-play.

In short. Stage 1b used to extract "worldbuilding and narrative claims," which on an actual-play transcript meant combat rolls, travel, and table talk were recorded as facts. The prompt was rewritten so a fact is standing setting knowledge only. Tested on a synthetic fixture and a real 89-minute actual-play VOD: session-play leakage fell from roughly 1 in 8 bullets to about 1 in 84, with no loss of genuine lore.

1 · The problem

The reading stage (Stage 1b) is the only point in the pipeline that invents content: for each segment of a transcript it produces claim bullets, which a human then approves into the wiki as facts. Its instruction told the model to extract "worldbuilding and narrative claims" and to "err on the side of over-inclusion."

On a tabletop actual-play recording that is the wrong target. A session is mostly play — dice rolls, combat, travel, out-of-character chatter — and only sometimes lore. The old prompt could not tell the difference, so it dressed up session events as facts: "a palace guard dealt 7 slashing damage," "the party rode north on the north road." None of that belongs in a worldbuilding wiki.

2 · The decision

Two principles were settled before touching the prompt.

A fact is standing setting knowledge. History, geography, peoples, factions, religions, powers, items, and the characters who inhabit the world — asserted by anyone (the GM, an NPC, or a player's character). In-world history counts. The session's own play-narrative does not.
Durable-state rule. When play changes the world, the extractor keeps the result, not the action. If the party assassinates the king, the fact is "King Theron is dead" — never "a player threw a dagger and rolled 23." Recapping the play-by-play is a separate concern (session summary), not fact extraction.

3 · How it was tested

A
A synthetic fixture for fast, controlled iteration An 8-segment actual-play transcript built so each segment is a deliberate test case: lore exposition, in-world history, an NPC rumour, player-character backstory, a kill that changes the world, and pure session-play (combat + dice, travel, out-of-character break) that must yield nothing.
B
A real 89-minute actual-play VOD as a reality check A genuine recorded session — messy auto-generated captions and all — to confirm the prompt generalises beyond the hand-built fixture rather than just "teaching to the test."

Original: ~10 of 32 bullets were session-play — all of segments 006/007 plus blow-by-blow detail smeared into 005 ("killed by Garrick's thrown dagger… struck in the throat").
Rewrite v1 killed the leakage but over-corrected: it stopped treating a player-character's own backstory as setting knowledge, dropping segment 004 from 4 facts to 2 and generalising "I was orphaned" into a vague "children were orphaned."
Rewrite v2 added character identity — including player-character backstory — explicitly back in scope, and forbade generalising away a fact's subject. Result: 19 bullets, every one genuine lore; all three play segments empty.

The prompt held up well on messy real captions. Long stretches of in-character chatter — a pre-session banter segment, a meandering conversation about love, idle table talk — correctly produced zero bullets, while it captured deep lore: the war that ruined magic, a catastrophic ritual 7,000 years ago, the golden dragon Oroxy who reigns over the Nexus, the history of the monastic knight order.

But a pattern the synthetic fixture never exercised slipped through: exploration and loot. The session is an expedition, and the model treated the party's adventuring as fact —

Leaked bullet (before tweak)After exploration-and-loot tweak
"Samples of metal and stone were collected from the ruined tower site."dropped
"The party previously investigated a location with a bridge…"dropped — survives only as context for a real lore claim
"A character possesses remnants of stone taken from the island."dropped
"Rift gave a magical language book as a reward for two days of work."still present — lone holdout

The exclusion list was extended to name what the party finds, collects, loots, or is given, plus party possessions and prior-session actions. Re-running both transcripts: 4 of 5 leaks fixed on the real VOD (84 bullets, ~1 borderline holdout), and no regression on the synthetic fixture — still 19 clean bullets.

What changed in the codebase:

  • stage1b.py — the extraction prompt rewritten around standing setting knowledge, the durable-state rule, and explicit exclusion of session-play.
  • _qa_fixtures/aldara-session/ — a new permanent QA fixture exercising the lore-vs-session-event distinction.
  • docs/pipeline/reading.md and CONTEXT.md — updated to define a claim and a session event and the boundary between them.

One residual is left deliberately: a single borderline quest-reward bullet on the real VOD. The pipeline's design already tilts toward over-inclusion — a spurious bullet costs a human reviewer seconds to reject — so chasing the last bullet would risk overfitting the prompt for no real gain.
